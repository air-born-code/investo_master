# Investo Master — Patience and Conviction Policy

**Adopted:** 2026-07-18  
**Status:** Governing investment principle

## Core principle

Investo Master conducts research every week, but investment decisions are expected to be extremely rare. We may study hundreds of companies and assets and wait two years or longer before finding one opportunity worthy of capital.

The normal weekly conclusion is:

> **No action. Continue learning and wait.**

The project is not judged by the number of ideas it produces, trades it proposes, or positions it changes. It is judged by whether it compounds understanding and improves the quality of the rare decisions that matter.

## Research cadence is not buying cadence

Weekly research exists to:

- Detect structural changes early.
- Build knowledge before an opportunity becomes obvious.
- Accumulate evidence over time.
- Track management, economics, competition, valuation, and risks.
- Recognise when facts diverge from market expectations.
- Be prepared when exceptional quality and exceptional price finally coincide.

Weekly publication must never create pressure to recommend a trade. We will not lower the investment bar because cash is idle, a report is due, markets are exciting, or no recent candidate has qualified.

Patience is an active part of the strategy, not an absence of work.

## What conviction means

Conviction is a conclusion earned through evidence, falsification, and repeated underwriting. It is not certainty. It is not excitement, familiarity, price momentum, stubbornness, or the size of an existing position.

Conviction must remain open to revision. New contradictory evidence receives more attention—not less—after a candidate becomes a favourite.

## The conviction ladder

Candidates move through the research system deliberately:

```text
Discovered
    ↓
Watchlist
    ↓
Researching
    ↓
High Conviction
    ↓
Waiting for Price / Decision Review
    ↓
Owned — only when confirmed by the human investor
```

At any point, a candidate may move to `Rejected` or `Thesis Broken`.

`High Conviction` is a research state, not an automatic buy instruction. A high-conviction candidate may remain on `Waiting for Price` indefinitely.

## Minimum gate for decision review

An opportunity cannot be labelled `Decision Review` until all of the following are documented:

1. **Falsifiable thesis** — a simple and specific explanation of why the investment should work.
2. **Primary evidence** — strong, dated evidence supporting the central claims.
3. **Variant perception** — a clear explanation of what the market may be underestimating or misunderstanding.
4. **Power-law potential** — a credible path to an extraordinary outcome, including what must be true for a 10× result.
5. **Durability** — evidence that the business or asset can defend its position and survive adverse periods.
6. **Management and incentives** — deep review of competence, integrity, ownership, governance, and capital allocation.
7. **Economics and financial resilience** — sound unit economics or a credible route to them, plus balance-sheet survival analysis.
8. **Disconfirming research** — a serious bear case built from the strongest contrary evidence.
9. **Valuation asymmetry** — scenario-based analysis showing adequate upside relative to permanent-loss risk and a margin for error.
10. **Pre-mortem** — a written explanation of how the investment could fail or permanently impair capital.
11. **Kill criteria** — observable facts that would invalidate or materially weaken the thesis.
12. **Monitoring plan** — the small set of metrics, events, and dates that matter after the decision.
13. **Position-sizing assessment** — a separate analysis of downside, fragility, liquidity, correlation, and personal circumstances.

Failure to satisfy any gate means more research, waiting for price, or rejection—not forced action.

## Concentration

When a truly exceptional opportunity clears every gate, meaningful concentration may be justified. Concentration is earned independently for each idea and must remain survivable if the thesis is wrong.

Business conviction alone does not determine position size. Position sizing must separately consider:

- Probability and severity of permanent capital loss.
- Balance-sheet and funding risk.
- Business-model fragility.
- Technological and regulatory risk.
- Liquidity.
- Correlation with existing exposures.
- Time horizon.
- Maximum tolerable drawdown and personal circumstances.

The project will never equate conviction with immunity from error. No single position should be capable of destroying the investor's ability to continue.

## Weekly reporting rule

Every weekly overview must state one explicit action posture:

- **No action** — no candidate currently justifies further decision work.
- **Continue research** — important questions remain unanswered.
- **Waiting for price** — thesis quality is high but expected return is inadequate.
- **Decision review required** — every research gate is complete and current conditions merit human consideration.

The report must make the distance between `interesting`, `high conviction`, and `actionable at this price` visually obvious.

## Safety boundary

Investo Master performs research and decision support. It does not execute trades, promise returns, or replace personalised financial, tax, or legal advice. The human investor retains responsibility for every decision.

## Operating maxim

> Study continuously. Wait without discomfort. Act rarely. When the evidence, asymmetry, and price align, act with earned conviction—then keep testing the thesis as if we might still be wrong.
