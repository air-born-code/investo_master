## Executive signal

The first live Investo Master run finds four genuine structural changes and five companies worthy of continued work, but no investment ready for decision review.

The strongest evidence is not in a speculative forecast. It is in orders, backlogs, contracts and physical capacity. Global electricity demand is forecast by the International Energy Agency to grow 3.6% annually through 2030, with data centres driving roughly half of the expected increase in US demand. GE Vernova booked $2.4 billion of data-centre electrification equipment orders in the first quarter—more than in all of 2025. Vertiv expects 29%–31% organic sales growth for 2026. The Age of Electricity is no longer merely a narrative.

The difficulty is price. GE Vernova and Vertiv are now valued at approximately $284 billion and $113 billion respectively. Using company guidance, that is roughly 40 times GE Vernova's 2026 free-cash-flow midpoint and 51 times Vertiv's. Those simple comparisons are not valuations by themselves, but they show that strong execution is already expected.

Two less-settled situations deserve concentrated research. Rocket Lab's proposed $8 billion acquisition of Iridium could create a rare end-to-end space platform spanning launch, spacecraft, spectrum and recurring services. It also introduces a $3.6 billion bridge commitment, stock issuance, regulatory approvals and integration risk while Neutron remains unproven. MP Materials is becoming a genuine US mine-to-magnet platform, but first-quarter economics included $42.3 million of government price-protection income. We must separate strategically valuable from independently profitable.

The correct Week 1 result is therefore **no action**. The database begins with two watchlist companies and three active research candidates. None passes the valuation and full-underwriting gates.

## Five things that matter now

### 1. Electricity demand has entered a different regime

The IEA forecasts global electricity demand growth of 3.6% a year between 2026 and 2030. US demand is expected to rise nearly 2% annually, more than twice the rate of the previous decade, with data centres responsible for around half of the increase. After roughly fifteen years of stagnation, demand in advanced economies is rising again.

This is broader than AI. Cooling, electric vehicles, industrial reshoring and electrification all contribute. AI is important because it adds concentrated, time-sensitive loads that require generation, transmission, transformers, switchgear, backup power and cooling at once.

**Investo Master inference:** the durable bottleneck is likely to be the ability to deliver reliable megawatts on a required site and schedule—not electricity as an abstract commodity. The research should follow equipment backlogs, connection queues, turbine slots, transformer lead times and energisation dates.

### 2. The obvious power beneficiaries are executing—and already recognised

GE Vernova reported first-quarter orders of $18.3 billion, up 71% organically, and backlog of $163 billion. Its combined gas-turbine backlog and slot reservations increased to 100 GW. Electrification booked $2.4 billion of data-centre equipment orders, more than the business booked in all of 2025.

Vertiv's first-quarter sales rose 30%, including 23% organic growth, while adjusted operating margin expanded 430 basis points to 20.8%. Management raised full-year organic growth guidance to 29%–31% and adjusted free-cash-flow guidance to $2.1–$2.3 billion.

The businesses are performing exceptionally. The stocks are priced accordingly. This is a useful baseline lesson: recognising a theme correctly after it becomes obvious is not enough. We need either a less-recognised value-chain beneficiary, a temporary dislocation, or evidence that the runway is materially longer and more profitable than current expectations.

### 3. AI connectivity has spectacular growth and spectacular concentration

Credo's fiscal-2026 revenue reached $1.335 billion, up approximately 206%, while operating cash flow reached $464 million. Its fourth-quarter GAAP gross margin was 68.2%. Efficient high-speed connections are essential as AI clusters scale.

The counterevidence is equally important. Two customers represented 49% and 32% of fiscal-2026 revenue, and the top ten represented roughly 90%. Share-based compensation was $182.6 million, while an at-the-market programme issued $736 million of stock during the year. At an approximate $38 billion market value, the company was valued at nearly 29 times fiscal-2026 revenue at the data cut-off.

Credo may be an exceptional company. The present evidence does not yet show an exceptional investment. The central test is whether new products and customers can diversify revenue before the leading deployments normalise.

### 4. Rocket Lab is becoming a different company

Rocket Lab's first-quarter revenue rose 63.5% to $200.3 million, gross margin reached 38.2%, and backlog exceeded $2.2 billion. The company then agreed to acquire Iridium for $54 per share in cash and Rocket Lab stock, representing approximately $8 billion of enterprise value.

If completed, the transaction adds Iridium's globally coordinated L-band spectrum, more than 2.55 million subscribers, a 500-plus partner ecosystem, and recurring communications services. Rocket Lab could design satellites, manufacture them, launch them and operate the service. That is strategically more interesting than a launch-only thesis.

But this is not a free option. Rocket Lab had a $45 million first-quarter net loss and used $50 million of operating cash. It raised approximately $446 million through an at-the-market offering in the quarter. The Iridium agreement is supported by a $3.6 billion 364-day bridge commitment and requires cash, stock, shareholder and regulatory approvals. Neutron's first flight is still expected later in 2026.

The next underwriting question is no longer “Can Electron and space systems grow?” It is: **What per-share value remains after permanent financing, integration, Iridium investment and Neutron execution?** Until the Form S-4 and financing plan answer that question, the valuation cannot be responsibly underwritten.

### 5. Rare-earth scarcity is real; MP's economics need decomposing

The IEA estimates that China represented 91% of magnet-rare-earth refining and 94% of sintered permanent-magnet production in 2024. Its new 2026 outlook says export controls and concentrated refining have turned a theoretical vulnerability into an immediate economic-security issue.

MP Materials increased first-quarter NdPr production 63% to 917 metric tonnes and sales volume 117% to 1,006 tonnes. Magnetics revenue reached $21.1 million as the company expands from mining and separation into finished magnets.

Government support materially changes the downside. A ten-year agreement provides a $110-per-kilogram price floor for eligible NdPr products, while the planned 10X magnet facility has an offtake backstop and minimum-EBITDA support. That can finance a strategically necessary supply chain that pure market prices might not support.

It also complicates valuation. First-quarter consolidated revenue was $90.6 million, price-protection income was $42.3 million, and the company reported an $8.0 million net loss. We need a bridge between reported results, government support, plant-level cash economics and the returns available to common shareholders.

## Candidate cards

### GE Vernova — watchlist

**Why it matters:** A scarce global platform spanning dispatchable generation, transmission equipment, grid systems and a large installed service base.

**Evidence for:** $18.3 billion of first-quarter orders; $163 billion backlog; 100 GW of gas-turbine backlog and slot reservations; $2.4 billion of data-centre electrification equipment orders.

**Evidence against:** Wind is expected to lose approximately $400 million of segment EBITDA in 2026. First-quarter net income was inflated by a $4.5 billion pre-tax M&A gain. At roughly $284 billion of market value versus a $7.0 billion midpoint for 2026 free-cash-flow guidance, much of the scarcity value is already recognised.

**Kill criterion:** Evidence that order strength reflects cancellable reservation activity rather than profitable demand, or that backlog margins fail to improve with pricing.

**Next decisive evidence:** Segment-level Q2 orders, backlog margin, data-centre order composition and turbine-slot conversion.

### Vertiv — watchlist

**Why it matters:** One of the most direct ways to supply power management and thermal infrastructure inside increasingly dense data centres.

**Evidence for:** First-quarter sales up 30%; Americas organic growth of 44%; adjusted operating margin of 20.8%; full-year organic-growth guidance of 29%–31%; free-cash-flow guidance of $2.1–$2.3 billion.

**Evidence against:** Approximately $113 billion of market value equates to roughly 51 times the 2026 free-cash-flow midpoint. The filing explicitly warns about customer order cancellation, fixed-price contracts, capacity expansion and categorical shifts in technology spending.

**Kill criterion:** A material decline in book-to-bill accompanied by cancellations, margin pressure or evidence that customers have over-ordered capacity.

**Next decisive evidence:** Q2 organic orders, backlog, book-to-bill, capacity ramp and working-capital behaviour.

### Credo — researching

**Why it matters:** Connectivity power and bandwidth are genuine constraints inside scale-out AI systems. Credo's active electrical cables, DSPs, retimers and SerDes intellectual property may occupy a valuable layer.

**Evidence for:** Fiscal-2026 revenue of $1.335 billion; fourth-quarter revenue growth of 157%; 68.2% quarterly gross margin; $464 million of fiscal-year operating cash flow.

**Evidence against:** Two customers generated 81% of annual revenue. Share-based compensation equalled $182.6 million, or 39% of reported net income. The approximate market value was close to 29 times fiscal-year revenue.

**Kill criterion:** Failure to diversify beyond the two leading customers before their deployment cycles slow, or persistent dilution that absorbs a disproportionate share of operating progress.

**Next decisive evidence:** Fiscal Q1 2027 customer mix, product mix, design wins outside the leading hyperscalers and diluted-share growth.

### Rocket Lab — researching

**Why it matters:** A successful combination with Iridium could form one of very few vertically integrated public space platforms, with scarce spectrum and recurring services layered onto launch and manufacturing.

**Evidence for:** Q1 revenue growth of 63.5%; $2.2 billion backlog; more than 70 contracted launch missions; Iridium's 2.55 million subscribers and L-band spectrum; founder-led management with an execution record in Electron.

**Evidence against:** $45 million Q1 net loss; $50 million operating cash outflow; roughly $446 million of quarterly ATM funding; a $3.6 billion bridge commitment; regulatory and integration risk; Neutron has not flown. The approximate $42 billion market value remains high relative to current revenue.

**Kill criterion:** A permanent financing package or share issuance that destroys per-share asymmetry, material Iridium customer attrition, or repeated Neutron schedule failures without corresponding risk reduction.

**Next decisive evidence:** The Form S-4, financing stack, pro-forma share count, Iridium investment requirements, approval timetable and verified Neutron milestones.

### MP Materials — researching

**Why it matters:** MP is building one of the few scaled, integrated rare-earth magnet chains outside China at a moment of genuine strategic scarcity.

**Evidence for:** Q1 NdPr production up 63%; sales volume up 117%; magnetics revenue up to $21.1 million; $1.74 billion of cash and short-term investments; ten-year government price and offtake mechanisms.

**Evidence against:** Q1 price-protection income of $42.3 million was almost half the size of reported revenue. Consolidated net loss remained $8.0 million. Magnet manufacturing must still scale, meet yields and qualify customers. Government contracts reduce commodity risk but add policy and execution dependencies.

**Kill criterion:** Failure to reach stable separated-product and magnet yields, large unplanned 10X capital overruns, or evidence that economics are unattractive outside government guarantees.

**Next decisive evidence:** Independence yields, 10X capital budget and milestones, commercial customer pricing, magnet qualification and ex-support return on invested capital.

## Secondary structural radar

### Advanced nuclear: improve the source map before selecting a stock

The Nuclear Regulatory Commission issued a new Part 53 licensing pathway in March 2026 and authorised TerraPower construction activity. The IEA expects nuclear generation to rise 2.8% annually through 2030, more than twice the 2021–2025 pace, but most near-term growth is outside the US and Europe.

The potential bottlenecks are fuel conversion, enrichment, qualified components, construction execution and financing—not merely reactor designs. No public candidate is promoted this week. A future deep week should map the nuclear fuel cycle and distinguish licensed, funded capacity from promotional project pipelines.

### Strategic minor minerals: possibly more asymmetric than the headline commodities

The IEA's 2026 critical-minerals work highlights gallium, germanium, tungsten, graphite, yttrium and magnet rare earths as highly exposed to concentration and export controls. These are small markets supporting far larger semiconductor, defence, aerospace and energy value chains.

Small markets can create extraordinary scarcity, but they can also be illiquid, opaque and dominated by uneconomic projects. The research opportunity is to find processors, by-product producers and recycling platforms with real feedstock, technical capability and customer qualification.

## What we are not doing

- We are not buying power-infrastructure stocks simply because their orders are growing.
- We are not annualising one exceptional quarter and calling it a valuation.
- We are not assuming Rocket Lab's Iridium transaction closes or creates value.
- We are not treating a government price floor as the same thing as a durable commercial moat.
- We are not promoting an advanced-nuclear developer before mapping licences, fuel, capital and construction risk.
- We are not interpreting a lower share price as proof that a security is cheap.

## Decision record

**Decision:** No action.

**Reason:** Structural demand is strongly evidenced, but the obvious beneficiaries carry demanding valuations. The less-settled candidates have material unanswered questions involving concentration, financing, integration, government support or unproven production scale. None has a complete bear case, scenario valuation, pre-mortem and kill-criteria monitoring record.

**What would change the decision:** A large price dislocation without thesis impairment; new evidence of underappreciated durability or economics; a financing structure that preserves per-share asymmetry; or discovery of a less-recognised bottleneck owner with strong balance-sheet protection.
